# The Utopia: The World After We Choose Life Over Death

Welcome to 2050, where humanity finally realized that being alive and healthy is actually more enjoyable than being dead or diseased.
I know, we're just as surprised as you are.

## How We Got Here: A Timeline of Surprisingly Good Decisions

### 2026: The 1% Miracle

It started with a simple idea: what if we spent [1% less on killing people](../solution/1-percent-treaty.md) and 1% more on keeping them alive?

The [global referendum](../strategy/global-referendum.md) hit 500 million signatures in six months.
Turns out, when you ask people "Would you like to not die of preventable diseases?" most of them say yes.
Who knew?

The defense contractors panicked until we showed them the [VICTORY Bonds](../economics/victory-bonds.qmd) returns.
40% annual yield?
Suddenly Lockheed Martin was very interested in making MRI machines instead of missiles.

### 2028: The 2% Revelation

Here's the funny thing about not dying: it's addictive.

Once people saw what \$27 billion in redirected military spending could do - [15 new treatments in the first year](../references.md#recovery-trial) - they wanted more.
The 2% Treaty passed even faster than the first one.

By 2028, we were spending \$54 billion annually on the [Decentralized Institutes of Health](../solution/dih.md).
Cancer survival rates jumped 20% in 24 months.
Diabetes? We found five new treatments that actually work.
Depression? Turns out it's easier to treat when you're not worried about nuclear war.

### 2030: The Cascade Effect

By 2030, something beautiful happened: countries started competing to redirect MORE military spending.

Norway: "We're doing 3%!"
Switzerland: "3.5%, and we're funding longevity research!"
Costa Rica: "Amateurs. We disbanded our military in 1948. We're putting 100% into health!"

Even the big military spenders couldn't resist.
When China announced 2.5%, the US had to match.
When Russia went to 3%, everyone else followed.
It was the first arms race where everyone wins.

### 2035: The Tipping Point

Global military spending: \$1.8 trillion (down from \$2.7 trillion)
Global health research spending: \$950 billion (up from \$68 billion)

The results?
- Cancer: 80% curable
- Heart disease: Preventable with a monthly shot
- Alzheimer's: Stopped in its tracks
- Aging itself: Slowed by 40%

The average human lifespan hit 95 years.
Not just living longer - living BETTER.
Your 90-year-old grandmother isn't in a nursing home; she's running marathons and starting her third career.

**9:00 PM:** You check the news (yes, we still have news, but it's mostly good).
